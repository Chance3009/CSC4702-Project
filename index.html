Project Overview

This project introduces the fundamental concepts of position and orientation in robotics, progressively adapted for three distinct educational audiences: junior school learners, high school students, and undergraduate-level learners.

The content moves from intuitive understanding to formal mathematical representation, explaining how robots understand direction, rotation, and spatial relationships.

Junior School

Intuitive explanations using compass directions and real-world analogies. No complex math required!

High School

Introduction to trigonometry, angles, and 2D rotation matrices with visual demonstrations.

Undergraduate

Advanced concepts including 4×4 homogeneous transformation matrices and frame composition.

Learning Objectives

  • Explain orientation intuitively without complex mathematics
  • Introduce 2D rotation using trigonometry and rotation matrices
  • Demonstrate 3D homogeneous transformations and coordinate frame composition
  • Bridge the gap between conceptual understanding and formal mathematical notation
  • Develop spatial reasoning skills through interactive visualizations

Educational Journey

This educational journey explores how autonomous systems determine their position, change their orientation, and move precisely through space, from basic concepts to advanced mathematical formulations.
