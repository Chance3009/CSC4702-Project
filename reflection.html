üéØ Outreach Intent & Educational Philosophy

This project explores how position and orientation can be taught to audiences with different technical backgrounds. The key challenge was adjusting abstraction levels while maintaining accuracy.

Using the same robot character (Robo) across all levels creates a continuous learning narrative. Mathematical rigor increases with audience level, but the core concept stays the same‚Äîmaking advanced ideas accessible without oversimplification.

üìä Audience Adaptation Strategy

üéØ Junior School

Topics Covered:

  • Understanding position as a location in space
  • Orientation as the direction a robot is facing
  • Pose as a combination of position and orientation
  • Basic directional concepts using everyday landmarks

Approach: Intuition-first using physical movement and visual cues. No formal math‚Äîshowing that strong foundations don't require equations.

üìê High School

Topics Covered:

  • Reference frames and directional vectors
  • Trigonometric representation of rotation
  • 2√ó2 rotation matrices and coordinate transformation
  • Pose representation and navigation accuracy in 2D

Approach: Bridging intuition with math as a practical tool. The warehouse scenario shows why orientation matters, not just how equations work.

üéì Undergraduate

Topics Covered:

  • Six Degrees of Freedom (6-DOF) in 3D space
  • 3D rotation matrices and axis-based rotations
  • 4√ó4 homogeneous transformation matrices
  • Pose composition and relative transformations

Approach: Formal mathematics aligned with real robotics practice. Visualization helps even advanced learners grasp abstract concepts.

üîç Critical Reflection

This project taught us that simplifying content isn't the same as dumbing down ideas. Younger audiences need less formalism, but concepts must stay accurate.

Learners responded best to visual, narrative-driven explanations. Effective robotics education needs both technical correctness and communication design.

üí° Key Insights

  • Visualization is Essential: Interactive elements make abstract concepts tangible
  • Storytelling Works: Continuous narrative maintains engagement
  • Progressive Complexity: Gradual math introduction prevents overload
  • Multiple Modes: Video, interaction, and text reinforce learning

  • Mastered robotics fundamentals: pose and transformations
  • Learned to communicate complex ideas to different audiences
  • Applied progressive abstraction principles
  • Improved teamwork and communication skills
